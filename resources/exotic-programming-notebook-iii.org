* Motivating Finding: I Ching Predicts EoC

** The discovery (2026-01-17)

We asked: do the M17 EoC sigils land in a predictable region of parameter space?

Using the I Ching hexagram framework as a lens:
- ䷀ 乾 (Qián, "The Creative"): pure yang, maximal intervention, high update-prob
- ䷁ 坤 (Kūn, "The Receptive"): pure yin, minimal intervention, low update-prob
- ䷊ 泰 (Tài, "Peace"): dynamic balance, creative serves receptive, mid-range params

We defined the "泰 zone" as: ~update-prob ∈ [0.3, 0.7]~ and ~match-threshold ∈ [0.3, 0.7]~.

* Edge of Chaos (EoC) Hypotheses

EoC = Edge of Chaos.

** CA / Exotype hypotheses

- EoC emerges when exotype rewrite pressure is strong enough to preserve diversity
  but not so strong that it collapses into “magma” or static attractors.
- EoC corresponds to exotypes that preserve multiple competing local motifs rather
  than enforcing a single attractor (metastable interactions).
- Phenotype coupling is a key dial: soft constraints (not hard resets) sustain EoC.
- Certain sigil families may cluster near EoC (controlled mix + low rotation + mid thresholds).
- Nomination gaps suggest EoC lives on a thin manifold; robustness across contexts
  is part of exotype “goodness.”

** CA / Exotype validation ideas

- Sweep `update-prob` × `mix-mode` × `match-threshold` to map the EoC manifold.
- Track stability across context schedules (lock-0/1/2) to quantify fragility.
- Compare EoC candidates by robustness to perturbations vs degeneracy rates.

* EoC ↔ Cyberants hypotheses

- Both domains reward sustained adaptability: EoC in CA parallels “stable survival
  + continued exploration” in ants.
- EoC sigils may encode “controlled perturbation” that maps to exploration–
  exploitation balance in ant policies.
- Robust EoC exotypes should generalize across contexts; analogously, cyberants
  should generalize across grid layouts and food distributions.
- EoC-driving morphisms could become policy adjustment operators (mode shifts,
  hunger/tau coupling), acting as regime shifters in ants.
- Caveat: some EoC sigils may amplify churn without utility; cross-domain checks
  should test correlation with survival/score improvements.

** EoC ↔ Cyberants validation ideas

- Pick a small EoC-exotype set and test against baseline AIF ants for survival.
- Measure transfer: do EoC sigils improve score stability across varied maps?
- Identify “busy but useless” cases where EoC ≠ ant utility.


* Mission 0 — Self-Transfer: CT-Native EoC Discovery

Goal: build MMCA-native xenotypes expressed in category theory that can
reliably find, recognize, and produce Edge of Chaos effects from any
starting configuration.

** The foundational problem

All other missions depend on transferring "good" patterns to external
domains (ants, music, Q&A, math). But what makes a pattern "good"?

Current answer: Joe inspects visually and knows EoC when he sees it.
Computational proxies (entropy, diversity) approximate this, but they're
only proxies.

For exotic programming to be a *method* rather than an art, we need:
1. Programmatic EoC recognition that matches human judgment
2. CT-formalized selection that guides any configuration toward EoC

** The self-transfer insight

Instead of: exotypes → external domain (ants) → xenotype evaluation
Do: exotypes → MMCA itself → CT-expressed xenotype evaluation

The xenotype for MMCA *is* MMCA — the system evaluates itself.
The CT formalism describes both the exotypes AND the evaluation criterion.

** Two layers to develop

*** Analysis layer: Recognizing EoC

Current proxies:
- Entropy (spatial, temporal)
- Motif diversity
- Lyapunov-like stability measures
- Regime classification (collapse, chaos, EoC, static)

What's missing:
- CT characterization of EoC (what morphism structure does it have?)
- Boundary detection (where exactly does EoC end and chaos begin?)
- Robustness measure (how stable is this EoC across perturbations?)

Hypothesis: EoC has a specific CT signature — perhaps it's where:
- Identity and projection are in convex combination (泰 tensor)
- Morphisms neither collapse nor diverge
- The category has "interesting" but finite structure

*** Program layer: Guiding to EoC

Current approach: evolutionary search, hope to land in 泰 zone

What's needed:
- Active guidance: given current state, which direction leads to EoC?
- Hexagram navigation: use 乾/坤/泰 structure to steer
- Gradient signal: not just "is this EoC?" but "how far from EoC?"

Analogy: PSR/PUR from P4NG (see ~/code/p4ng/main.tex), specialized to CT and MMCA

- *PSR (Pattern Selection Record)*: "Which pattern do we select, and why now?"
  Captures the current intention, the chosen pattern, and the reasons (not just the outcome).
  In MMCA: "given this sigil set + state, we selected these exotypes because X"
  We can also note alternatives considered (the other wiring/hex options).

- *PUR (Pattern Use Record)*: "How did it go when we applied it?"
  Captures outcomes + evidence, plus what it implies for future use.
  In MMCA: "we applied these exotypes, observed Y dynamics, and should adjust Z"

Together they form a learning loop. Crucially:

*"Rule 110 all the time" produces EoC in the phenotype but is anti-EoC
at the genotype level.* We want EoC across ALL levels — genotype,
phenotype, exotype, xenotype. The PSR must capture WHY a selection was
made, not just that it locally produces interesting dynamics.

This means Mission 0 isn't just "find EoC" but "find EoC-producing
*processes* that are themselves EoC-like" — creative selection, not
static rules.

*** Bootstrap mode: Hexagrams as xenotype for octagrams

The inversion: I Ching doesn't compete with sigils — it *judges* them.

#+begin_src
Octagram (8-bit) → exotype → MMCA dynamics
                                 ↓

                                 ↓
                    Hexagram-based fitness signal
                                 ↓
                    Guide octagram evolution

How it works:
- Octagrams are the exotype layer (256 raw sigils, random mutations)
- Hexagrams are the xenotype layer (64 semantic patterns that evaluate)
- An octagram producing 泰-like dynamics gets boosted
- An octagram producing 否-like dynamics gets penalized
- Pure 乾 (collapse) or pure 坤 (frozen) gets filtered

Why this helps:
- Don't need all 64 hexagrams interpreted — just the diagnostic ones
  (泰, 否, 乾, 坤, and a few key transition patterns)
- Ancient framework as evaluation criterion for modern search
- Tests whether hexagram structure captures something metrics miss

**** The lift: sigils → exotypes → hexagrams

Bootstrap phase uses hexagrams as fixed evaluators. Next step:
learn to *lift* octagram dynamics to hexagram classifications.

#+begin_src
sigil (8-bit) → exotype params → MMCA dynamics → hexagram label
     ↑                                               ↓
     └─────────── learned mapping ←──────────────────┘
#+end_src

If we can reliably lift sigils to hexagrams, we've learned:
- A compression: 256 sigils → 64 hexagram classes
- Semantic meaning: each class has 乾/坤/泰 interpretation
- Transfer structure: hexagram relationships predict sigil relationships

This is the PSR/PUR loop for exotypes:
- PSR: "We selected sigil X because it maps to hexagram Y"
- PUR: "Sigil X produced dynamics Z, which matches hexagram Y (or not)"
- Learning: refine the sigil→hexagram mapping based on evidence

**** The 6×6 matrix structure

The 36-bit exotype has natural structure:
- LEFT (8 bits): spatial input from left neighbor
- EGO (8 bits): self-state
- RIGHT (8 bits): spatial input from right neighbor
- NEXT (8 bits): temporal projection
- PHENOTYPE (4 bits): 3 parents + 1 offspring

** Turing's 1951 Vision

From "Intelligent Machinery, A Heretical Theory":

"What we want is a machine that can learn from experience...
[machines] might still be made to rival [humans] in chess.
This... could be done fairly easily by... letting them play
against each other... with a mechanism for intercommunication.
They could ... converse with each other to sharpen their wits."

This is exactly the xenotype framework:
- Agents play against each other (ant war, Q&A competition)
- Intercommunication (shared knowledge base, Q&A threads)
- Sharpening wits (evolution toward better patterns)

** Kolmogorov connection

The arrow work (M9.5/M9.6) directly supports this:
- Terms as knowledge representations
- Normalization as inference
- Mining arrows from corpora (Stack Exchange → formal patterns)

Q ⊢ A in Kolmogorov's sense: a well-posed question *proves* its answer.
The agent's job: learn to pose well-posed questions.

** Koans as curriculum

"Stepping stones, starting from simplest possible tasks and growing in
complexity" (Arthur & Polak, SFI 2004):

1. Trivial koans: pattern matching, simple lookup
2. Basic inference: one-step deduction
3. Multi-step: chaining inferences
4. Creative: generating new questions
5. Discovery: questions that yield novel theorems

Hexagram transitions map the curriculum:
- 坤 (receptive learning) → 泰 (dialogic practice) → 乾 (creative contribution)

** Implementation path

1. *Fulab adapter* (immediate)
   - Mirror cyberant adapter for Fulab architecture

   - Simple koans as initial content

   - Stack Exchange → formal representations
   - Argument modeling, semantic structure
   - Feed into agent knowledge bases

4. *Heuristic reasoning* (T2 from LEAPQA)

Reward: If it works, we have:

- A path to Turing's vision of self-improving AI
- Potential for genuine mathematical discovery
- Validation that "patterns of improvisation" are universal
